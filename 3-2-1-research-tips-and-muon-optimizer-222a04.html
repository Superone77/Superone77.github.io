2 Paper Introduction

I. How to Read a Paper

The paper outlines a practical and efficient method for reading research papers, known as the three-pass approach :

  1. First Pass (Skimming): This involves quickly going through the paper to get a general idea of its content. The reader should focus on the title, abstract, introduction, section headings, and conclusion. This pass helps determine whether the paper is relevant to their interests or research 2.
  2. Second Pass (In-depth Reading): In this stage, the reader engages more deeply with the material, paying attention to figures, tables, and key technical sections. They may also try to understand the core contributions and methodology, but not necessarily every detail.
  3. Third Pass (Critical Review): This is a detailed and critical examination of the paper. The reader should be able to reconstruct the paper's arguments, evaluate its strengths and weaknesses, and consider potential improvements or further research directions.

II. Muon: An optimizer for hidden layers in neural networks

Muon is a new optimizer for neural network training that improves speed and stability by combining momentum with fast matrix orthogonalization via the Newton-Schulz iteration. Applied mainly to hidden layer weights, Muon enhances learning efficiency with minimal overhead (<1%), outperforming AdamW and Shampoo in training speed and scalability. It achieves state-of-the-art results on tasks like CIFAR-10 and NanoGPT, and scales well to models with over 1.5B parameters. Muon offers a practical, theoretically grounded approach to faster deep learning.
